Route cap & the repricing cliff

The route cap is a hard limit (unlike the soft transaction caps) - adding a route is a billing/structural event, so blocking "create route #6 on Starter -> upgrade" is appropriate and won't disrupt daily work.

  • 5 routes Starter = 5 x 2,500 = PKR 12,500 / month
  • 6 routes Enterprise = 6 x 4,000 = PKR 24,000 / month (one route approximately doubles the bill)

Mitigate the cliff with a warm "you've outgrown Starter" upgrade flow, a short transition discount, or Custom volume pricing for large route counts.

Upgrade path

  • In-app upgrade with proration - keep all data, unlock features and lift limits instantly.
  • Nudge to upgrade when a route hits ~80% of a Starter limit, or tries to open a locked feature.
  • Custom is sales-assisted for many routes or bespoke limits/modules.

Why Enterprise is the obvious choice

  • Unlimited core volume for clients, invoices, recovery, and vouchers.
  • P&L + Analytics turn raw data into decisions.
  • Cheque management solves a genuine distributor pain.
  • Automated recovery with WhatsApp/SMS reminders + aging.
  • Unlimited staff logins, longer history, and Excel exports.

Implementation notes

  • Map each row to a feature or limit in the entitlement model.
  • max_routes is a hard limit; transaction caps should be soft.
  • Keep numbers tunable and consider an annual plan with 2 months free.
